Afternoon
🛬 Land · BSNL SIM · Hotel Prepaid taxi ₹250–350
First three things at airport: (1) Buy BSNL SIM at the booth inside terminal — ₹150–200 with data. Only network that works on all 3 islands. (2) Screenshot your pre-booked ferry tickets. (3) Take prepaid taxi to hotel — booth near exit, no bargaining needed. App cabs don't work here. Once at hotel: connect to WiFi and download offline Google Maps for Havelock and Neil right now.
5:00 PM
🌊 Corbyn's Cove Beach — First Touch Swim FREE · Auto ₹80
4 km south of Aberdeen. White sand, calm waves, safe swimming. Don't rent chairs from vendors (₹100+) — the sand is perfect. Just walk in barefoot. Your body needs to meet this ocean before anything else happens. 45 min.
6:00 PM
🚩 Flag Point — Panoramic Harbour Sunset Sunset 1 FREE
3-min walk from Corbyn's Cove. Hilltop viewpoint over Port Blair harbour, Ross Island channel, and open sea. The sun drops behind layered island silhouettes. Arrive by 6 PM for the full colour range — it peaks around 6:20 PM in late April. Leave before dark. This is sunset #1 of your trip.
7:30 PM
🍛 Aberdeen Bazaar — Dinner Food ₹120–300 / person
Best spots: Annapurna (pure veg thali ₹120 — always crowded, always fresh), New Lighthouse Restaurant (grilled seafood ₹200–350), Icy Spicy (fast casual ₹100–150). Walk the bazaar after. Buy nothing yet — Neil market prices are 30–40% cheaper.
9:00 PM
📋 Tonight's Admin (30 min)
Ask hotel to arrange a cab to Wandoor tomorrow (₹400–500 total for 5). Confirm Jolly Buoy permit is sorted (via hotel or agent — book the night before). Charge everything. Set alarm for 5:45 AM.

Jolly Buoy Island — Living Coral Sanctuary

"One of India's finest coral reefs. No plastic. Pack your own food."
5:45 AM
🎒 Wake · Pack Day Bag
Carry today — strictly: Reusable steel/glass water bottle (NO plastic — checked at Wandoor), snacks in tiffin box or cloth bag, reef-safe sunscreen (regular sunscreen kills coral, ₹10,000 fine — buy Raw Elements or wear a rash guard instead), towel, change of clothes in dry bag, original ID card, ₹2,500 cash, motion sickness tablet (take 30 min before boarding).
7:00 AM
🚗 Drive to Wandoor Jetty (30 km south) Cab ₹400–500 total
45-min scenic coastal drive. Must reach Wandoor by 7:45 AM — boat departs 8:30 AM with a daily cap on visitors. Buy forest permit at the counter: ₹75 / person. Total package (permit + boat + return): ₹1,200–1,800 / person depending on operator. Hack: Pre-arrange permit via hotel the night before to skip the queue.
8:30 AM
🚤 Boat to Jolly Buoy (35–45 min) Coral World
The boat ride itself is stunning — emerald channels, open sea, dense jungle shorelines. Sit at the bow or upper deck. Island is a protected zone inside Mahatma Gandhi Marine National Park. Closes at 2 PM sharp — all visitors must be back on the return boat by then.
9:15 AM
🐠 Coral Reefs · Glass-Bottom Boat · Snorkeling Glass boat ₹200 · Snorkel ₹1,000–2,500
Living coral in water shallow enough to see from shore — clownfish, parrotfish, anemones, giant clams. Glass-bottom boat ride (₹200 extra) — mandatory for non-swimmers, extraordinary for everyone. Guided snorkeling: confirm availability on the day (forest dept sometimes restricts). 2.5–3 hrs on the island. White sand, dense jungle, zero development. Eat your packed lunch under the trees. Do NOT touch coral — ₹10,000 fine, strictly enforced.
1:30 PM
🚤 Return Boat · Back to Port Blair · Rest
Arrive Wandoor ~2:30 PM. Tender coconut from stall (₹40). Back in PB by 3:30 PM. Late lunch / early dinner at Aberdeen (₹150–200). Full rest tonight — 4 AM departure tomorrow for North Andaman. Set alarm for 3:15 AM. Pack your bag for 2 nights away.
💚 Jolly Buoy Hacks
  • Book permit the night before via hotel agent — saves queuing at Wandoor and guarantees entry within the daily cap.
  • Bring your own snorkel mask if possible — saves ₹200–300 rental and it'll be a better fit for hours in the water.
  • Reef-safe sunscreen only — wear a full rash guard instead if you can. Protects you AND the coral.
  • Shared cab to Wandoor for 5 people = ₹400–500 total. Much cheaper than private booking (₹1,200+).

Cellular Jail · Chidiya Tapu Sunset · Light & Sound Show

"History, forest, and the most powerful show in India."
9:00 AM
🏛️ Anthropological + Fisheries Museum Context ₹20 each
Andaman tribal history — Jarawa, Onge, Sentinelese. After Jolly Buoy's coral ecosystem experience, this context lands differently. 1 hour total. Walk Aberdeen Bazaar for any last shopping after.
11:00 AM
⛓️ Cellular Jail (Kala Pani) ⭐ 4.7 Must-Do ₹30 entry
Open 9 AM–noon and 1:30–5 PM (Tue–Sun). 700 solitary cells in a radial design — prisoners could see each other's wings but never reach them. India's freedom was built on the suffering documented here. Walk every corridor. Read every plaque. This is the most emotionally heavy place of the entire trip. Let yourself feel it. Photography allowed throughout. 2 hours minimum.
1:30 PM
🍽️ Lunch + Rest ₹150–250
Mandalay Restaurant or Gagan near Aberdeen. Rest 1.5–2 hrs at hotel. You need recovery time before the evening. Cab to Chidiya Tapu pre-arranged for 3:30 PM pickup.
3:30 PM
🚗 Depart for Chidiya Tapu (45 km south) Cab ₹1,500–2,000 return
45-min drive south through forests and coconut groves. Negotiate one round-trip rate. Same road as Wandoor (yesterday) but further south. Arrive by 4:15 PM.
4:15 PM
🌳 Chidiya Tapu Biological Park — Forest Walk Birds ₹25 entry
⭐ 4.4. "Bird Island" — 40 hectares of dense primary jungle. Endemic species found nowhere else on Earth: Andaman woodpecker, hill myna, Andaman drongo. Walk slowly, silently. No mobile signal — you're forced to be present. The forest sounds here are extraordinary. 45-min walk.
5:30 PM
🌅 Chidiya Tapu Sunset Point Sunset 2 FREE
Southern tip of South Andaman. Forest on three sides, the Andaman Sea spread out before you. The sun dissolves behind a line of islands. Birds go quiet. The sky moves through gold into deep orange into violet. This sunset is different from Flag Point — it's a forest sunset, not a harbour sunset. Stay until the first stars appear. Leave by 7 PM — dark arrives fast.
8:45 PM
🔦 Cellular Jail Light & Sound Show Powerful ₹75–100 / person
English show 8:45 PM (Hindi at 7:30 PM — too tight after Chidiya Tapu). Pre-book at andamantourism.gov.in — sells out on weekends. The jail walls are lit and the voices of freedom fighters narrate their story. One of the most spine-tingling cultural experiences in India. Sleep in silence after. Wake at 3:15 AM tomorrow. North Andaman awaits.

North Andaman — Ross & Smith Islands

Days 4–6 · Apr 30 – May 2 · Overnight loop from Port Blair
320 km from PB. Only road access — 10–12 hrs each way through the Andaman Trunk Road and the Jarawa reserve. This requires 2.5 days: drive Day 4, islands Day 5, return Day 6. Non-negotiable timing. Worth every hour.
⚠️ Read This Before Day 4
  • 4 AM departure is non-negotiable — arrive Diglipur by 4–5 PM to catch evening rest before the island visit.
  • Road passes through the Jarawa Tribal Reserve — no stopping, no photography, convoy rules apply. Different from Baratang (you're not stopping there).
  • Forest permit for Ross & Smith: obtained from Forest Office at Diglipur on Day 4 arrival evening or Day 5 morning. Bring original ID. Cost: ₹50 / Indian adult.
  • No ATM in Diglipur. Carry ₹3,000–4,000 cash per person for these 2 days.
  • Pre-book your overnight stay in Diglipur/Aerial Bay before leaving PB.

Ross & Smith Islands — The Twin Island Sandbar

"Two islands connected by a natural sandbar that appears and vanishes with the tide."
7:30 AM
📋 Forest Office Permits (if not done yesterday) ₹50 / person
Forest Office Diglipur opens ~8:30 AM. Get permits. Then drive 6 km to Aerial Bay Jetty. Speed boats operate 8 AM–2 PM. Aim to be on the first boat of the day — fewer people, calmer morning light on the sandbar.
8:30 AM
🚤 Speed Boat to Ross & Smith (25 min) ₹2,000 for 6-seater
Hire a speed boat from Aerial Bay Jetty. ₹2,000 for a 6-person boat. 25-min ride across stunning turquoise water. Arrive at Smith Island side first. The sandbar connecting Ross and Smith is the main spectacle — at low tide it emerges as a crescent of white sand with turquoise sea on both sides. Check the tide in advance — sandbar visible only at low tide. Aim for your visit during low tide window for the iconic sandbar walk.
9:00 AM
🏝️ Walk the Twin Islands + Sandbar Iconic
Spend 4–5 hours. The sandbar is the moment — stand on it with ocean on both sides, the two forested islands flanking you. Crystal clear gem-green water. Bring your own snorkeling gear (no rental here) — the reef around the islands has excellent visibility and marine life. Forest trail on Smith Island to the viewpoint. Bamboo huts and basic changing rooms on Smith. No food stalls — carry everything. Sand castle opportunity: the sandbar sand is perfect.
2:00 PM
🚤 Return to Aerial Bay + Lunch + Rest
Last boats leave ~2 PM. Return to Diglipur hotel. Late lunch, rest. This was the highlight of North Andaman. The evening is yours — optional: walk to Kalipur Beach at dusk for sea turtle spotting (nesting season ends March but adults still visible). Sleep early — another long drive tomorrow.

💚 North Andaman Hacks
  • Book the private cab to Diglipur 2–3 days in advance. Don't try to find one on the morning of departure. Call Andaman Cabs: 09933263867 or ask Zostel to arrange.
  • Carry packed snacks, a thermos of chai, and a neck pillow for the 12-hr drive. It's a long haul but the scenery makes it worth it.
  • Snorkel gear in your own bag — no rental at Ross & Smith. Even a basic ₹400 mask from a Port Blair shop makes the island 10× better.
  • The Dhaninallah Mangrove Walkway near Rangat is a free and beautiful 30-min stop en route — only if you have time buffer on the drive.
  • Check the low tide time for May 1 (use "Tides Near Me" app) and aim for your Ross & Smith boat to arrive during the low-tide window so the sandbar is fully exposed.

🌅 Radhanagar Beach (Beach 7) — Sunset ⭐ 4.8 Sunset 3 FREE
40-min scooty ride. Asia's best beach. Blue Flag certified. White sand, forest backdrop, no development in sight. Arrive by 5 PM — swim for an hour (safe flagged zone), then wait as the sun drops into the ocean. Hack: Walk 400m north from the main crowd for your own private beach. Stay until the first stars appear. This is the sunset that ruins all other sunsets forever. Return by 7:15 PM for kayaking.
7:30 PM
🌟 Night Kayaking — Bioluminescent Waters Unmissable ₹1,200–1,500 / person
Book through Barefoot India, India Kayaks, or resort desk. Paddle at night through water that erupts in electric blue with every stroke — bioluminescent plankton activated by movement. Splash the water: it ignites. The effect is strongest on dark nights (check moon phase — new moon = brightest glow). Dark sky, stars overhead, glowing ocean below. One of the most surreal experiences available to a human being. No phone. No talking. Just paddle. Returns ~9:30 PM. Sleep immediately.

Kalapathar Sunrise · Scuba · Sea Walk · Ferry to Neil

"Up before dawn. Down to 12 metres. On a ferry by 2 PM."
5:15 AM
🌅 Kala Pathar Beach — Sunrise ⭐ 4.7 Sunrise 1 FREE
6 km east, 15-min scooty ride in the dark. Arrive before 5:30 AM — the beach will be empty. Black volcanic rocks, white sand, the Bay of Bengal cracking open in full orange light. Stay until 6:30 AM. 20-min pool briefing + full gear + PADI guide. 9–12m depth, 40-min underwater. You'll see: coral gardens, moray eels, giant clams, lionfish, nurse sharks (harmless). Complete silence. Complete weightlessness. The coral world from the inside. GoPro rental ₹500–800. Mention any ear/sinus issues honestly — safety first.

🚶 Underwater Sea Walk (Helmet Dive) Sea Floor ₹2,500–3,500 / person
Walk on the actual sea floor at 5–7m with a pressurized helmet. Normal breathing. No swimming skill needed. 25–30 min. Completely different experience from scuba — you walk between fish and coral like a visitor in their world. Hack: Book scuba + sea walk with same operator = 15–20% combo discount. Rest 30 min after, eat well, hydrate.

💚 Neil Island Hacks
  • Everything tide-dependent. Check "Tides Near Me" app each morning. Natural Bridge, Laxmanpur flats, Sitapur caves — all need low tide. Your day plan follows the ocean's schedule.

  • No ATM on Neil. Carry all your cash from Havelock or Port Blair.
