Port Blair (Sri Vijaya Puram)

Days 1–3 · Apr 27–29 · History, Heritage & the Baratang Wilderness

🏠 Stay — Port Blair (3 nights)

Apr 27 (Sun) — Arrival & First Breath

Easy landing. Let the islands find you.

🛬 Land at Veer Savarkar Airport Arrival
Prepaid taxi or auto to Zostel (~₹250–350). Check in. Get a BSNL SIM at the airport — it's the only network that works reliably across all islands. Download offline Google Maps for Havelock and Neil before you lose signal.

🌊 Corbyn's Cove Beach Grounding
10 min ride. First contact with Andaman water. Walk barefoot. Let the ocean recalibrate you. Watch the sky change. No agenda — just arrival. This sets the tone for everything that follows.

🍛 Aberdeen Bazaar — Dinner & Explore Food
Annapurna (veg thali ₹120), New Lighthouse Restaurant (fresh seafood ₹250–400), or street dosas. Walk around. Buy nothing yet — wait for Neil for better prices.
9:30 PM
🌙 Zostel Café — Meet Your Travel Community Social
The rooftop café is your best networking spot. Everyone here is a traveler. Introduce yourselves. Many solo travelers are actively looking for a group like yours. ALSO: Book your Baratang taxi tonight for tomorrow's 3:30 AM departure — ask the Zostel staff or front desk.
DAY
2

Apr 28 (Mon) — Baratang: Cave, Forest, Earth

3:30 AM departure. The most surreal day of your trip. (Cellular Jail is closed Mondays — this is why Baratang is here.)
🌿 A pre-dawn drive through India's last tribal reserve, a speedboat through cathedral mangroves, and caves sculpted over millennia. Approach this day with complete presence — it's unlike anything else in India.
3:30 AM
🚗 Depart Port Blair VERY EARLY
Private taxi for 5 = ₹4,500–6,000 total (≈₹900–1,200/person split). Or join a shared tour at ₹2,800–4,000/person. Carry: water, snacks, mosquito repellent, light jacket, torch, ID cards, offline map.
6:00 AM
🌳 Jarawa Tribal Reserve Convoy Sacred Silence
Vehicles move in a police-escorted convoy. No stopping, no photography, no interaction — this is law, and it genuinely protects one of Earth's last isolated peoples. Windows down, forest rushing past in the dark. You may see Jarawa people on the roadside. Witness in silence and humility.
8:30 AM
🚤 Mangrove Speedboat + Limestone Caves Transcendent
Speedboat ₹900/person from Nilambur Jetty. The boat carves through narrow mangrove tunnels — a green cathedral, roots weaving overhead, light filtering through. Then a 2km forest trek to caves of white stalactites and stalagmites. Nature's architecture. The silence inside the cave is absolute.
11:00 AM
🌋 Mud Volcano (if time allows) Rare
India's only mud volcano — the earth bubbles softly. Shared jeep from jetty. 20 minutes; skip if you want to leave earlier for the return convoy.
12:30 PM
🍚 Lunch + Return to Port Blair (arrive ~3:30 PM) Long Day
Simple fish thali near Baratang jetty. Long drive back through forest. You'll be back by mid-afternoon. Rest. Book your Havelock ferry tonight if you haven't already (makruzz.com or via Zostel).
DAY
3

Apr 29 (Tue) — Kala Pani + Islands + Light Show

India's history lives here. Feel its full weight.
9:00 AM
⛓️ Cellular Jail (Kala Pani) History
Entry ₹30. Open 9 AM–12 PM, 1:30–4:45 PM. 700 solitary cells. Walk corridors alone — let the silence talk. India's freedom fighters were sent here to die. The most emotionally heavy place you'll visit on this trip. Photography allowed. 2 hours minimum.
11:30 AM
🏛️ Anthropological + Fisheries Museum Culture
Both free or ₹20. Andaman tribal cultures — Jarawa, Onge, Sentinelese — exhibited here. Context for what you experienced in the forest yesterday. Walk Aberdeen Bazaar after for lunch.
2:30 PM
🏝️ Ross Island + North Bay Island Water & Ruins
Ferry from Phoenix Bay Jetty (~₹600 combo). Ross Island: British administrative HQ reclaimed by jungle — deer walk freely among collapsed churches. North Bay: water sports hub — glass-bottom boat ₹400, snorkeling ₹600, sea walk ₹2,500, jet ski ₹800. Choose 2–3 activities. Return ferry by 5:30 PM.
7:30 PM
🔦 Cellular Jail — Light & Sound Show Must-Do
Hindi 7:30 PM, English 8:45 PM. ₹75–100. Book online in advance. The jail walls are lit up; voices of freedom fighters fill the night air. One of the most viscerally moving experiences in India. Pack bags after this — tomorrow is Havelock. Sleep early.
⛴️ Ferry: Port Blair → Havelock (Day 4 morning, Apr 30)

Makruzz / Sea Link: ₹1,000–1,200/person · 90 min · Departs Phoenix Bay ~6–10 AM. Book at makruzz.com AT LEAST 7–10 days before travel. Government ferry: ₹350–450, ~3 hrs. Arrive Havelock by 8–10 AM.

Apr 30 (Wed) — Arrival + Radhanagar at Sunset

Asia's best beach. One sunset. It will silence you.
8:30 AM
🛥️ Arrive Havelock Jetty Check-in
Auto to Hostel Nemo (~₹100–150). Check in. Rent 3 scooties. Fill tank at the petrol pump immediately. Ask hostel for Night Kayaking booking for Day 5 evening — books up fast.
10:30 AM
🏖️ Vijaynagar Beach (Beach No. 5) — Morning Swim Open Sea
The turquoise here is ridiculous. Shallow, safe, calm. Swim out to the buoy lines. Build those sand castles. The water is warm, clear, and completely still in the morning. Floating face-up is a meditative act in itself.
1:00 PM
🍛 Lunch + Rest Siesta
Fat Martin's, Anju Coco Grove, or Full Moon Café. Fresh catch, coconut rice. Avoid noon–3 PM heat. Hammock time. Nap is mandatory.
4:30 PM
🌅 Radhanagar Beach (Beach No. 7) — SUNSET Life-Changing
⭐4.8 · 40-min scooty ride. Blue Flag certified. Asia's best beach. White sand, forest canopy behind, not a building in sight. Arrive by 4:30 PM — swim (flagged safe zone), walk, lie down. Then wait. The sun sets at the ocean here. The sky turns colors that don't have names. Stay until the stars come out. This is the arrival gift the Andamans give every traveler.
DAY
5

May 1 (Thu) — Kalapathar Sunrise + Elephant Beach

The most meditative morning. The most electric afternoon.
🌅 Set your alarm for 5 AM. Ride alone (or together) in the dark to Kalapathar. Sit on a black rock. Watch the sun tear open the Bay of Bengal. That silence — the ocean just waking up — is your morning meditation.
5:30 AM
🌅 Kala Pathar Beach — Sunrise Meditation Sacred
⭐4.7. Black volcanic rocks, white sand, zero tourists at dawn. 6 km from jetty area. The contrast of dark rock against turquoise water is otherworldly. Sit, breathe, watch the horizon change. 45–60 min of pure presence. One of the most serene places in India.
9:00 AM
🤿 Elephant Beach — Water Sports Active
⭐4.5 · Open 7:30 AM–3:30 PM. Speedboat from jetty ₹900/person return. Snorkeling ₹500, banana boat ₹400, parasailing ₹1,200, jet ski ₹600, glass-bottom boat ₹400. Pick 2–3 activities. Living coral reefs in shallow water — clownfish, parrot fish, the occasional reef shark below. Bring your own underwater camera if possible.
2:00 PM
🌴 Village Ride + Café Crawl Explore
Scooty through the island interior — coconut groves, paddy fields, local fishing villages. Stop for tender coconut (₹40). Discover something not on any list. Rest 3–5 PM — the evening is big.
7:00 PM
🌟 Night Kayaking — Bioluminescent Waters Unmissable
₹1,200–1,500/person. Book through Barefoot India, India Kayaks, or your hostel. Paddle at night through water that glows electric blue with every stroke — bioluminescent plankton activated by movement. Splash the water, it lights up. Dark ocean, Milky Way above, glowing sea below. This is one of the most spiritually disorienting, beautiful moments available to a human being. Do not skip this. Do not be on your phone.

May 2 (Fri) — Scuba Diving + Final Radhanagar

Go 12 meters deep. Come up fundamentally changed.
🤿 Scuba on Day 6 (May 2) = 4 full days before your May 6 flight. The 18–24hr no-fly rule is satisfied with a massive safety margin. ✅
8:00 AM
🤿 SCUBA DIVING Deep Ocean
Barefoot Scuba (Beach 3), Dive India, or Infinity Scuba — all PADI certified, reputable. Beginner fun-dive: ₹3,200–4,000/person (1 dive, ~45 min underwater, 9–12m depth). 20-min training included. You'll see: coral gardens, moray eels, giant clams, lionfish, and possibly a nurse shark. Silence. Weightlessness. The deep ocean is the most meditative place on Earth. BOOK 24 HRS IN ADVANCE.
1:00 PM
🍽️ Long Lunch + Rest Recovery
Diving is physically and emotionally draining in the best way. Eat well. Drink lots of water. Talk about what you saw. Everyone's experience underwater is different — compare notes. Rest 2–4 PM.

🌅 Radhanagar — Final Sunset on Havelock Farewell
Go one last time. The sea will look different from the top now that you've seen it from below. Stay until the stars appear. If you've made friends from the hostel (and you will have), come here as a group. This is where Havelock friendships are sealed.
9:00 PM
🌙 Pack Bags. Tomorrow: Neil Island. Wind Down
Return scooties by 9 PM tonight or first thing tomorrow. Ferry leaves early. Exchange contacts with anyone you want to stay in touch with.

⚡ Things Nobody Tells You

  • BSNL SIM is non-negotiable. Jio/Airtel barely work outside Port Blair. Buy at airport or Aberdeen Bazaar on Day 1.
  • Offline Maps first thing. Download Google Maps offline for Port Blair, Havelock, and Neil before you leave Wi-Fi range. You will get lost otherwise.
  • Book all ferries before you travel. makruzz.com — at least 7–10 days ahead. April–May is still peak season. Ferries fill up, especially Port Blair → Havelock.
  • Carry cash always. ₹5,000–8,000 minimum. UPI and cards fail regularly. ATMs exist only reliably in Port Blair. Havelock has one (often empty).
  • Only ONE petrol pump on Havelock. Zero on Neil (govt shops, close 5 PM). Fill every time you can. This is a real logistical issue — don't get stranded.
  • Cellular Jail is CLOSED Mondays — this is why Baratang is Day 2 in this plan. Don't try to change this without accounting for Monday.
  • Laxmanpur Natural Bridge only appears at LOW TIDE. Use the "Tides Near Me" app or ask your SeaShell Neil concierge for the daily tide schedule. Plan accordingly.
  • April–May heat is intense. 32–35°C. Mandatory rest 12–3 PM every day. Carry ORS/electrolyte sachets, SPF 50+ sunscreen, head scarf, and 1.5L water at all times.
  • Resort booking: Book SeaShell Neil directly via seashellhotels.net or call +91 95319 07002. Limited rooms — do this as soon as possible.
  • The best things on these islands have no entry fee. Sunrises, sunsets, tidal walks, fishing village roads. Slow down and find them.
